Overview

Economic inequality: The uneven distribution of wealth (e.g., income) among individuals or groups within a society.

  • How: How do living in a unequal society affect citizens’ sociopolitical minds?
  • How → When: How does the macro-level inequality reach the micro level?
  • When → What if: What does the exposure to inequality changes?

Economic inequality is bad

Social scale：Risk taking behaviors ↑; homicides ↑; life expectancy ↑; infant mortality ↑; obesity ↑; Interpersonal trust ↓; child well-being ↓; education performance ↓; imprisonment rates ↓; teenage births ↑ (Cozzolino 2011; Payne, Brown-Iannuzzi, and Hannay 2017)

Political scale: Democratic engagement ↓; Representation ↓; Responsiveness ↓ (Bartels 2008; Gilens 2014; Schlozman, Verba, and Brady 2013; Solt 2008)

Social volcano: Economic inequality ↑ → social unrest ↑ → shaking regime stability↓ (He 2003)

People can feel inequality in various ways (Jin, Hu, and Meng 2024)

Impartial governance:

  • The damage of economic disparities on political equality in democracies has been well documented in the literature (Schlozman, Verba, and Brady 2013).
  • Formal and informal institutions exist in non-democracies to convert economic resources into political influence and favorable treatment.
  • Wealthy Chinese private entrepreneurs form extensive connections with local government officials to gain privileges (Chen and Dickson 2010).

Government Non-responsiveness:

  • Substantial evidence indicates that economic inequality reduces social capital and generates distrust and social conflicts.
  • Class conflict theory suggests that in highly unequal societies, people’s perceptions of economic positions on the social ladder are accentuated (Kraus, Tan, and Tannenbaum 2013).
  • Low levels of social capital and accentuated social conflicts make governance difficult as Baldwin and Huber (2010) showed that income differences make an agreement over which public goods to provide more difficult, therefore have a negative relationship with public goods provision (Epp and Jennings 2020).

Judicial Injustice:

  1. greater inequality implies that the well-off have more resources to control the legal apparatus while the poor are more disadvantaged.
  2. the advantaged are motivated to attempt such control because of intensified social conflicts.

Anti-Corruption Inadequacy:

When inequality leads to widespread corruption, citizens doubt governments’ intentions and abilities to combat corruption and perceive governments as incompetent to address the problems of the system.

When → What if

Comparison → Perception

There exists, in the human organism, a drive to evaluate his opinions and abilities. The person will do this by comparing himself with others. (Festinger 1954, 117)

  • Upward comparison (vs. the rich) → discontent (Crosby 1976; Gurr 2015)
  • Downward comparison (vs. the poor) → self-esteem (Wills 1981; Hopkins and Bilimoria 2008; Gibbons and Gerrard 1989)

Comparison Effects

\(H_{1.1}\): Upward comparison → redistribution support ↑

\(H_{1.2}\): Downward comparison → redistribution support ↓

Institutional conditions

\(H_{3.1}\): Governmental and State owned enterprises (SOE) employees → lower redistribution support
\(H_{3.2}\): The rest → higher redistribution support

Experimental Design (Inspired by Condon and Wichowsky 2020)

[In Chinese] Given the social ladder on the left, please imagine the individuals in Chinese society who are the (worst/best) off, (level 1/10)—those who have the (least/most) wealth, (lowest/highest) education level, and (least respected/most respected) jobs. Specifically, consider how you differ from these individuals in terms of your own income, educational background, and job status.

Policy preferences:

Which of the following policies can help alleviate social and economic inequality and promote common prosperity? Please rank the policies below in order of importance (You don’t have to select all):

_______ Increasing the minimum living allowance (Dibao)
_______ Raising retirement pensions
_______ Raising unemployment benefits
_______ Increasing the amount of education loans

Participants in the control group will be asked to imagine conversing with individuals who have similar income, education levels, and social status as themselves, and to reflect on what topics might arise in such a conversation.

Preliminary conclusions

  • Economic inequality changes people’s mind of the society and power
  • The effects conduct through daily sociopolitical observations and feelings.
  • Easier exposure to inequality changes people’s policy preferences, conditioned by their relationship with the institution—the microfoundation of the redistribution reformation
