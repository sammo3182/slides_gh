Civic quality of political discussion

Criteria (Friess and Eilders 2015)

  • Rationality
  • Interactivity
  • Equality
  • Civility
  • Constructiveness
  • Topic relevance refers to the coherence on the topic, ensuring that the discourse remains focused and pertinent. Linguistic complexity is a composite variable that combines logical complexity and lexical complexity, providing an assessment of the sophistication and depth of the arguments presented.
  • Interactivity: captures the dynamic exchange between participants, reflecting a dialogic environment that is essential for understanding and consensus (Friess and Eilders 2015)
  • Equality: all participants have an equal opportunity to contribute, preventing domination by a few public opinion leaders (Jurgen Habermas and Rehg 1998; Friess and Eilders 2015)
  • Civility: pertains to the respectfulness and politeness of interactions (Friess and Eilders 2015).

Determinants

  • Institutional (Aarts and Thomassen 2008; Dahlberg, Linde, and Holmberg 2015; Ezrow and Xezonakis 2011; Linde and Erlingsson 2013; Magalh√£es 2016)
  • Cultural (Dewey 1954; J√ºrgen Habermas 1998; Warren 2017)
  • Psychological

Psychological Persona

Personality on Quality

  • Agreeableness & neuroticism have positive effects on rationality and equality,
  • Openness & extraversion have negative effects on rationality, equality, and civility
  • Conscientiousness has positive effect on interactivity

They tend to produce content that is sensitivity and less similar with official media, and hold Right-leaning ideologies

  • the sensitivity of the content being posted,
  • the similarity of the posted content to that of official media,
  • the prevalence of offensive or uncivil content
  • the ideology tendency held by the individuals.

Conclusion

  • Openness and extraversion do not contribute to the quality of political discussion as expected
  • Strong traits leads to more civil discussions.
  • Empowerment is at least partially true.
