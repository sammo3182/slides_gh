An eigenvector is a special vector that, when multiplied by a matrix, only changes in scale (gets longer or shorter) but keeps pointing in the same direction.

Imagine you have a transformation (like a stretch, rotation, or reflection) represented by a matrix. When you apply this transformation to most vectors, they change direction. But eigenvectors are special - they only get stretched or shrunk while maintaining their original direction.
